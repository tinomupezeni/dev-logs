# Agentic Orchestration vs. Coding

**Concept:** Shifting the engineer's identity from the "Builder" (typing syntax) to the "Architect" (managing intent and verification).

## The Shift
- **Legacy Coding:** 90% Syntax/Debugging, 10% Architecture.
- **Agentic Orchestration:** 10% Prompting/Review, 90% Systems Design/Verification.

## Key Principles

### 1. Requirements Engineering
The AI is a "Precision Tool." If your instructions are vague, the result is "AI Spaghetti." You must be exact about the **Flow**.

### 2. Verification as the Premium Skill
In a world of "instant code," the ability to **prove** the code is correct is the highest-value skill. Master:
- Observability (Logging/Traces)
- Automated Testing
- Smoke Testing

### 3. The "Black Box" Audit
Never treat AI code as a "Black Box." Use the "Reverse Engineering Path" to ensure you understand the critical failure points of your system.

## Case Study: Shipwright
Shipwright was built without a single line of manual Rust code, yet it functions as a distributed systems platform because the **Intent** and **Infrastructure Awareness** were provided by the human Architect.
