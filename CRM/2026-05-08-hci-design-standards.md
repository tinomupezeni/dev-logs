## Summary
Establishment of Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) standards to guide the development of the CRM for high-density, informal markets like Eastgate Mall. The focus is on reducing cognitive load, bridging action gulfs, and creating inherent product virality.

## Design Foundations

### 1. Minimizing the Gulf of Execution
- **Strategy:** "The 3-Second Sale." Core transactions must be completed in 3 taps or less.
- **Application:** Use a 'Fast-Tap Grid' for top products on the landing screen. Implement 'Haggling Sliders' for real-time price adjustments.

### 2. Bridging the Gulf of Evaluation
- **Strategy:** Optimistic Feedback.
- **Application:** Immediate success animations/haptics upon 'Save', with background synchronization. Explicit status for 'Offline Mode' to build user confidence in data safety.

### 3. Recognition over Recall
- **Strategy:** Cognitive Offloading.
- **Application:** Use product visual cards and icons instead of search-heavy navigation. Limit critical dashboard information to  \pm 2$ items to prevent memory bottleneck.

### 4. Environmental Robustness (WCAG)
- **Strategy:** POUR Principles (Perceivable, Operable, Understandable, Robust).
- **Application:** 
    - **High-Contrast Themes:** Optimized for outdoor/direct sunlight usage.
    - **Large Targets:** Minimum 48x48px targets for one-handed, high-mobility operation.
    - **Physical Metaphors:** Use the 'Digital Notebook' mental model to ease the transition from paper.

## Referral & Viral Strategy
- **Status Signal:** Professional branded receipts shared via WhatsApp. This creates a 'Translation' success where the trader's professional image is enhanced, leading to organic peer-to-peer referral.
- **The Profit Story:** Visual 'Business Health' summaries that traders can easily show others to demonstrate the system's value.
